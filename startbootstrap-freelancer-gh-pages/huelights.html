Very Weak

This colour displayed on the Hue Lights signifies that the entered password received a score of 0/4 and therefore was classed as a 'Very Weak' password.

Tip: Avoid dictionary words, your password may be in the 100 most popular password!

Weak

This colour displayed on the Hue Lights signifies that the entered password received a score of 1/4 and therefore was classed as a 'Weak' password.

Tip: Avoid sequences of numbers (Ex: 12345) instead try placing numbers inside of words!

Ok

This colour displayed on the Hue Lights signifies that the entered password received a score of 2/4 and therefore was classed as an 'Ok' password.

Tip: Try adding multiple words with no correlation (Ex: "Soup" and "Elbow")

Strong

This colour displayed on the Hue Lights signifies that the entered password received a score of 3/4 and therefore was classed as a 'Strong' password.

Tip: Special characters are very useful for creating stronger passwords, you can use them to replace letters (Ex: 'A' = @)

Very Strong

This colour displayed on the Hue Lights signifies that the entered password received a score of 4/4 and therefore was classed as a 'Very Strong' password.

Tip: Longer passwords are always stronger.
